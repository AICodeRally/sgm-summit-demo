# New Hire and Onboarding Policy

**Policy Code:** SCP-016
**Category:** HR Policies
**Framework Area:** New Hire/Onboarding
**Status:** TEMPLATE
**Legal Review Required:** No

---

## PURPOSE

This policy establishes the framework for onboarding new sales representatives into Henry Schein's compensation plans, defining ramp schedules, training requirements, quota phase-ins, and commission eligibility during the initial employment period.

## SCOPE

This policy applies to:
- All newly hired sales representatives eligible for variable compensation
- All sales roles transferred from non-sales positions
- All Henry Schein divisions and business units
- Internal promotions to sales roles requiring compensation plan transition

## DEFINITIONS

**New Hire:** Any sales representative in their first 180 days of employment in a variable compensation role.

**Ramp Period:** The defined timeframe during which a new hire's quota and/or commission rates are adjusted to account for learning curve and territory development, typically 90-180 days.

**Quota Ramp:** Progressive increase in sales quota from reduced levels to full quota over the ramp period.

**Training Period:** The initial phase (typically 30-60 days) focused primarily on product training, systems training, and territory familiarization.

**Full Productivity Date:** The date on which a new hire transitions to 100% quota and standard commission rates.

---

## RAMP SCHEDULE

### Standard Ramp Schedule

New sales representatives follow a progressive ramp schedule:

**Month 1 (Days 1-30):**
- Quota: 0% of territory quota (training period)
- Commission: Eligible for commission on any closed deals at standard rates
- Focus: Product training, systems training, territory review, customer introductions

**Month 2 (Days 31-60):**
- Quota: 25% of territory quota
- Commission: Standard rates apply
- Focus: Initial customer meetings, pipeline building, first deals

**Month 3 (Days 61-90):**
- Quota: 50% of territory quota
- Commission: Standard rates apply
- Focus: Active selling, pipeline management, first quota attainment

**Month 4 (Days 91-120):**
- Quota: 75% of territory quota
- Commission: Standard rates apply
- Accelerators: Not eligible until Month 5

**Month 5+ (Days 121+):**
- Quota: 100% of territory quota (Full Productivity Date)
- Commission: Full plan including accelerators
- Status: Standard plan participant

### Accelerated Ramp (Experienced Hires)

For sales representatives with:
- 3+ years industry experience, OR
- Previous Henry Schein employment in sales role, OR
- Transfer from competitor in same vertical

**Alternative Schedule:**
- Month 1: 50% quota
- Month 2: 75% quota
- Month 3+: 100% quota (Full Productivity Date)

Approval required from:
- Hiring Manager
- Sales Operations Director

### Extended Ramp (Complex Territories)

For highly technical or complex territories (e.g., specialty medical equipment, large enterprise accounts):
- Month 1: 0% (training)
- Month 2-3: 25% quota
- Month 4-5: 50% quota
- Month 6: 75% quota
- Month 7+: 100% quota

## COMMISSION ELIGIBILITY

### During Training Period (Month 1)

New hires are eligible for commission on:
- Any deals they personally source and close
- Split credit (per Split Credit Policy) if assisting on existing opportunities
- SPIFs and contests (if explicitly included in program rules)

New hires are NOT eligible for:
- House accounts assigned to territory (unless they actively manage)
- Pipeline inherited from previous rep (unless they close the deal)
- Residual/renewal commissions from prior rep activity

### Ramp Period Commission (Months 2-4)

Full commission eligibility at standard rates on:
- All new business closed
- Upsells and cross-sells to existing customers
- Renewals where rep actively engaged customer
- House account activity if rep manages relationship

### Quota Attainment Bonuses

Quota attainment bonuses (e.g., quarterly/annual kickers) are calculated based on:
- Ramped quota, not full territory quota
- Example: Rep at 50% ramp quota who achieves 60% of full quota = 120% attainment for bonus purposes

## TERRITORY ASSIGNMENT

### Initial Territory Assignment

New hires receive:
- Defined geographic territory or account list
- Access to CRM with full account history
- Introduction package with top 20 accounts
- Pipeline report showing active opportunities

### Inherited Pipeline

**Opportunities in progress** from previous rep:
- Month 1-2: 50% commission to new rep, 50% to departing rep (if applicable)
- Month 3+: 100% commission to new rep if they close the deal

**Closed deals awaiting implementation:**
- Commission to departing rep
- New rep may earn residual/renewal commission when eligible

### House Accounts

Large strategic accounts may remain "house accounts" during ramp:
- Rep receives reduced commission rate (e.g., 50% of standard) during ramp
- Rep transitions to full commission after demonstrating competency
- Sales Manager provides coverage during initial period

## TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

### Mandatory Training (Month 1)

All new sales reps must complete:

**Week 1:**
- Company orientation
- Product catalog overview (40+ hours)
- Systems training (CRM, CPQ, ordering)
- Compliance training (HIPAA, anti-kickback, etc.)

**Week 2-3:**
- Territory deep-dive with manager
- Top 25 customer introductions
- Ride-alongs with senior reps (minimum 5 days)
- First solo customer meetings

**Week 4:**
- Sales process certification
- Product line certification (core products)
- First deal submission

### Ongoing Training (Months 2-4)

- Weekly manager 1-on-1s
- Monthly product training sessions
- Quarterly business reviews
- Peer mentorship program

### Certification Requirements

To transition to full quota (Month 5+), rep must demonstrate:
- Product knowledge certification (80% score on assessment)
- CRM proficiency (all opportunities logged correctly)
- Pipeline coverage (minimum 3x quota in pipeline)
- Customer satisfaction (no major complaints during ramp)

Failure to meet certification: Ramp period extended by 30-60 days with manager approval

## COMPENSATION DURING RAMP

### Base Salary

- Same base salary as fully ramped reps in same role level
- No reduction during training/ramp period
- Paid biweekly per standard payroll schedule

### Commission Payments

- Paid on same schedule as tenured reps (e.g., monthly)
- No draws or guarantees during ramp (unless specified in offer letter)
- Advances available per standard Draws & Guarantees Policy

### Draws & Guarantees (If Applicable)

Some roles may include guaranteed commission during ramp:
- Specified in offer letter only
- Typical range: $2,000-$5,000/month for first 90 days
- Recoverable or non-recoverable per offer terms
- Subject to Draws and Guarantees Policy (SCP-008)

## PERFORMANCE EXPECTATIONS

### Minimum Performance Standards (During Ramp)

**Month 2:**
- Minimum: 10% of ramped quota (2.5% of full quota)
- Target: 50% of ramped quota (12.5% of full quota)

**Month 3:**
- Minimum: 25% of ramped quota (12.5% of full quota)
- Target: 75% of ramped quota (37.5% of full quota)

**Month 4:**
- Minimum: 50% of ramped quota (37.5% of full quota)
- Target: 100% of ramped quota (75% of full quota)

### Performance Review & Consequences

**Failure to meet minimums:**
- Month 2: Manager coaching, action plan developed
- Month 3: Formal performance improvement plan (PIP)
- Month 4: Consideration for termination if below minimum

**Exceeding targets:**
- Recognition in team meetings
- Potential for accelerated ramp (early transition to 100% quota)
- Commission paid at standard rates (no penalty for exceeding ramped quota)

## SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES

### Mid-Quarter Start Dates

If hired mid-quarter:
- Quota prorated based on days remaining in quarter
- Commission eligibility begins on start date
- Ramp schedule begins on start date (not quarter start)

### Leaves of Absence During Ramp

If new hire takes leave (FMLA, medical, etc.) during ramp period:
- Ramp schedule pauses during leave
- Upon return, rep resumes at same ramp stage
- Example: Rep completes Month 2, takes 30-day leave, returns to Month 3 ramp

### Termination During Ramp

**Voluntary resignation:**
- Commission paid on all closed deals per standard policy
- No commission on pipeline opportunities
- Training costs not recovered

**Involuntary termination:**
- Commission paid on deals closed before termination date
- No commission on pending deals
- Company may recover training costs if within first 90 days (per offer letter)

## INTERNAL TRANSFERS

### Sales to Sales Transfers

When rep transfers from one sales role to another:

**Same product line, different territory:**
- No ramp period
- Immediate 100% quota in new territory
- Standard commission rates apply

**Different product line, same territory:**
- 30-day training period (0% quota Month 1)
- Month 2+: 100% quota on new product line
- May maintain commission on old product line during transition

**Different product line, different territory:**
- Standard ramp applies (Months 1-4)
- Manager discretion to accelerate based on experience

### Non-Sales to Sales Transfers

Internal transfers from non-sales roles (e.g., customer service, operations):
- Treated as new hire
- Standard ramp schedule applies
- May receive preferential consideration for accelerated ramp if internal product knowledge strong

---

## MANAGER RESPONSIBILITIES

Sales managers must:

**Week 1:**
- Provide territory overview and top account list
- Schedule customer introduction meetings
- Assign peer mentor
- Review compensation plan in detail

**Month 1:**
- Daily check-ins for first 2 weeks
- Weekly 1-on-1s thereafter
- Attend first 3-5 customer meetings with rep
- Provide CRM/systems access and training

**Months 2-4:**
- Weekly 1-on-1s reviewing pipeline, deals, questions
- Monthly performance review against ramp targets
- Identify coaching needs and provide training
- Approve quota adjustments if needed

**Month 5 (Full Productivity):**
- Final ramp review meeting
- Certification approval
- Transition to standard rep management cadence
