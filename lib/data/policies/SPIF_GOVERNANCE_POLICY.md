# SPIF & PLAN KICKER GOVERNANCE POLICY

**Policy Code:** SCP-006
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** APPROVED
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026

---

## PURPOSE

Establish governance standards for SPIFs (Sales Performance Incentive Funds) and plan kickers to ensure ROI, budget control, and strategic alignment.

---

## SPIF DEFINITIONS

**SPIF:** Extra commission opportunity beyond base plan
- Time-limited (typically 2-12 weeks)
- Specific objectives or deal types
- Additional payout on top of regular commission
- Requires approval and ROI justification

**Plan Kickers:** Built into base plan
- Examples: Multi-year kicker, New Logo bonus
- Not separately approved SPIFs
- Part of standard compensation plan
- Included in plan design

---

## SPIF TYPES AT JAMF

### 1. Multi-Year Deal SPIF/Kicker

**Definition:** Contracts 24+ months in duration

**Payout:** 2% of total bookings for multi-year contracts
- New multi-year deal: Standard payout
- Renewed multi-year deal: Same payout (incentivize long-term renewal)

**Eligibility:** AEs, SAEs, KAMs, Managers involved in deal

**Budget:** Projected based on multi-year deal pipeline
- Tracked quarterly
- Adjusted annually

**Approval:** Part of standard plan (no separate approval needed)

### 2. New Logo SPIF/Kicker

**Definition:** First-time customer to JAMF

**Payout:** Bonus beyond regular commission
- Recognizes higher effort for new customer acquisition
- Amount: Defined in plan document (typically 1-2% of bookings or fixed dollar amount)

**Eligibility:** New Logo AEs and supporting reps (SE, CS if involved)

**Budget:** Included in New Logo rep plan projections

**Approval:** Part of standard plan (no separate approval needed)

### 3. Segment Conversion SPIF

**Definition:** Converting existing customer to higher-tier product/plan

**Payout:** Extra quota retirement credit (not cash)
- Example: $50K conversion = $50K quota credit at $.50/$1 ratio = $25K credit
- Incentivizes upsell and product expansion

**Eligibility:** Account managers and AEs involved in expansion

**Budget:** Controlled via quota retirement metrics
- Limits on total quota retirement per rep/quarter

**Approval:** Tracked via quota system, no separate SPIF approval

## SPIF APPROVAL AUTHORITY

### Approval Thresholds

| SPIF Amount | Authority | Timeline | Requirement |
|------------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| <$50K | VP Sales + VP Comp | 5 days | Brief justification |
| $50K-$250K | CRB approval | 10 days | Business case, ROI, budget impact |
| >$250K | CRB + CFO + CSO | 15 days | Detailed business case, ROI, exec brief |

### SPIF Submission Requirements

**Every SPIF Requires:**
1. SPIF ID and description
2. Target audience and eligibility
3. Duration (start and end dates)
4. Objectives and success criteria
5. Payout structure and per-participant max
6. Total budget and funding source
7. ROI projection (minimum 3:1 projected, 2:1 actual)
8. Approver signature(s)
9. Tracking and measurement plan

---

## ROI MEASUREMENT

**Projected ROI (at approval):**
- Minimum 3:1 ROI (for every $1 of SPIF payout, $3 revenue)
- Example: $100K SPIF budget should generate $300K+ incremental revenue

**Actual ROI (post-SPIF):**
- Measured 30-60 days after SPIF ends
- Minimum 2:1 ROI (for every $1 spent, $2 revenue generated)
- If ROI <2:1: Lessons learned documented, improvements noted

**Tracking:**
- SPIF revenue tagged in CRM
- Comparison to control group (non-SPIF periods)
- Documented in CRB decision log

## SPIF BUDGET CONTROL

### Annual Budget

**Total SPIF Budget:** Approved annually (typically October for following year)
- Percent of base compensation budget (e.g., 5-10%)
- Allocated by quarter
- Cannot exceed annual amount without SGCC approval

### Quarterly Management

**Quarterly SPIF Budget Tracking:**
- Proposed SPIFs logged by VP Sales
- Running total against quarterly limit
- Once limit reached: No new SPIFs without exception approval
- Monthly reporting to SGCC

### Multi-Year Impact

**SPIF impacting multiple years:**
- Example: Multi-quarter SPIF
- Budget deducted from all affected quarters
- Reported to SGCC with full impact analysis

---

## CONFLICT AVOIDANCE

### Conflicts to Avoid

**SPIF conflicting with base plan:**
- Example: SPIF for same objective as plan metric
- May cause over-incentivization or cannibalization
- Review committee ensures complementary, not conflicting

**Multiple SPIFs for same rep:**
- Limit to 2-3 concurrent SPIFs per rep
- Avoid overwhelming or confusing messaging
- Ensure each SPIF targets different behavior

**SPIF vs. Compensation Plan Change:**
- Don't use SPIF to patch broken plan
- If plan adjustment needed: Follow plan change process
- SPIF is supplemental, not corrective

### Conflict Resolution

**If conflict identified:**
- CRB reviews and recommends modification
- Options: Reduce SPIF, modify plan, delay SPIF
- Decision documented with rationale

---

## VENDOR-FUNDED SPIFs

**Definition:** Third-party (vendor, partner) funds SPIF

**Requirements:**
- Written agreement with vendor/partner
- Clear payout terms and timing
- Company maintains control of eligibility/approval
- Participant disclosure (reps informed of vendor funding)
- No conflicts of interest

**Approval:**
- Vendor agreement reviewed by Legal
- SPIF approval process followed (amounts >$50K require CRB)
- Funding commitment verified before launch

---

## SPIF COMMUNICATION

**Rep Communication:**
- Announced at least 2 weeks before start
- Clear objective and payout structure
- Eligibility and examples
- Tracking and reporting frequency
- Contact for questions

**Manager Communication:**
- Brief on SPIF mechanics
- How to track participant progress
- How to answer rep questions
- Forecast impact on numbers

---

## SPIF DOCUMENTATION & TRACKING

**SPIF Registration:**
- Unique SPIF ID
- Approval documentation
- Participant list (updated weekly)
- Objective tracking
- Payout calculation
- Results/ROI analysis

**Record Retention:** 7 years (per Data Retention Policy)

## SPIF EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Multi-Year SPIF
- Objective: Increase contract term length
- Payout: 2% of bookings for contracts 24+ months
- Duration: Year-round
- ROI: Expected 5:1 (multi-year deals higher value/retention)

### Example 2: New Logo SPIF
- Objective: Accelerate new customer acquisition
- Payout: $5K per new customer in Q1
- Duration: 12 weeks (Q1)
- Budget: $200K (estimated 40 new logos)

### Example 3: Segment Conversion SPIF
- Objective: Upsell to higher-tier products
- Payout: $50K conversion = $25K quota credit
- Duration: 90 days
- Budget: $500K quota retirement
- ROI: $500K credit drives $2M+ expansion revenue

---

## REJECTED SPIF PROPOSALS

SPIFs typically rejected if:
- ROI <3:1 projected
- Conflict with existing plan/SPIF
- Unclear objective or measurement
- Lack of budget
- Timing inappropriate
- Legal/compliance concerns
