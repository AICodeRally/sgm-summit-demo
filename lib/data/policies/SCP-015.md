## PURPOSE
These Standard Terms & Conditions apply to all Henry Schein sales incentive compensation plans unless specifically modified in a particular plan document. They are incorporated by reference into each plan document.
## 1. EMPLOYMENT RELATIONSHIP
### 1.1 At-Will Employment
Participation in any incentive compensation plan does not alter the at-will nature of your employment. Your employment may be terminated by you or by the Company at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice. Nothing in this plan or any plan document creates an employment contract or guarantee of continued employment.
### 1.2 No Guarantee of Earnings
Participation in an incentive plan does not guarantee any specific level of compensation. Actual incentive earnings depend entirely on performance achieved, as measured according to the specific plan terms. The Company makes no representations or guarantees regarding potential earnings under any plan.
### 1.3 Not Part of Base Salary
Incentive compensation is variable pay and is not considered part of base salary for any purpose, including but not limited to: - Calculation of severance payments - Retirement plan contributions - Overtime calculations (where applicable) - Other benefit calculations
Unless explicitly stated otherwise in a specific benefit plan or policy.
## 2. PLAN ADMINISTRATION & MODIFICATION
### 2.1 Company Discretion
The Company retains sole and absolute discretion in: - Designing, implementing, and administering incentive plans - Interpreting plan terms and resolving ambiguities - Making all final decisions regarding eligibility, performance measurement, and payouts - Determining appropriate corrective actions for errors or unusual circumstances
### 2.2 Right to Modify or Terminate
The Company reserves the right, in its sole discretion, to: - Modify any incentive plan at any time, including during a performance period - Terminate any incentive plan at any time - Change plan terms, quotas, measures, payout structures, or any other plan element - Substitute different measures or components - Adjust for changes in business strategy, market conditions, or organizational structure
Notice of Changes: The Company will provide reasonable notice of material plan changes when practicable, except where: - Immediate action is required due to legal, financial, or business circumstances - Changes are necessary to correct errors - Changes are made to comply with law
Effect of Changes: - Prospective changes apply to performance periods beginning after the change effective date - Retroactive changes may be made to correct errors, comply with law, or address extraordinary circumstances
### 2.3 Plan Interpretation
The Compensation Review Board (or designated administrator) has final authority to interpret plan terms and resolve disputes. All interpretations and decisions are final and binding.
In the event of any conflict between: - Plan documents and communications: Plan documents control - Different versions of plan documents: The official version on file with Compensation controls - Plan terms and these Standard Terms & Conditions: These Standard Terms & Conditions control unless explicitly stated otherwise in the plan
## 3. ELIGIBILITY & PARTICIPATION
### 3.1 Eligibility Requirements
To be eligible for any incentive plan, you must: - Hold an eligible position as defined in the plan - Be actively employed on the date compensation is earned (as defined in the plan) - Not be on a final written warning or Performance Improvement Plan, unless otherwise approved - Comply with all Company policies and standards of conduct - Meet any other eligibility requirements specified in the specific plan
### 3.2 Continuous Active Employment Required
Unless otherwise specified in a plan document, you must be actively employed on the date incentive compensation is earned and on the date it is paid to receive payment. “Actively employed” means: - Not on unpaid leave of absence (except legally protected leave where required) - Not under notice of termination (given or received) - Performing the duties of your position
### 3.3 Suspension or Termination of Eligibility
The Company may suspend or terminate your eligibility to participate in any incentive plan if: - You violate Company policy - You engage in misconduct - You are placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (at Company discretion) - You are under investigation for policy violations or misconduct - Your performance falls below acceptable standards - You fail to meet eligibility requirements
## 4. PERFORMANCE MEASUREMENT & QUOTAS
### 4.1 Performance Data
Data Sources: Performance is measured using Company-approved data sources and systems. The Company determines which data sources are authoritative.
Data Accuracy: While the Company strives for accurate data, participants are responsible for: - Reviewing performance statements - Promptly reporting any suspected errors or discrepancies - Maintaining accurate records to support inquiries
Data Corrections: The Company may correct data errors at any time, including after payments have been made.
### 4.2 Quota Assignments
Quotas are assigned at the Company’s sole discretion based on territory potential, historical performance, market conditions, corporate objectives, and other factors.
Quota Changes: The Company may adjust quotas at any time for reasons including but not limited to: - Territory changes - Account reassignments - Changes in market conditions - Organizational restructuring - Correction of errors - Changes in business strategy
No Guarantee: Quotas are goals, not guarantees. The Company does not guarantee that quotas are achievable, though quotas are generally designed to be achievable through strong performance.
### 4.3 Performance Period
Performance is measured over defined performance periods as specified in each plan. Performance periods may be: - Modified by the Company - Extended or shortened - Combined or separated
## 5. PAYMENT TERMS
### 5.1 Earning vs. Payment
When Earned: Incentive compensation is legally “earned” under applicable law when [jurisdiction-specific definition - consult legal counsel]. For purposes of this plan, incentive is generally earned when: - Performance has been achieved - Performance has been verified - All eligibility requirements are met - The earning event specified in the plan has occurred (e.g., customer order booked, payment received, service delivered)
When Paid: Earned incentive compensation is paid according to the payment schedule in each specific plan, subject to these Terms & Conditions.
### 5.2 Payment Method
Incentive payments are made through: - Regular payroll (subject to standard payroll processing timelines) - Or separate payment process as specified in the plan
All payments are made in U.S. Dollars unless otherwise specified.
### 5.3 Tax Withholding
All incentive payments are subject to: - Federal income tax withholding - State and local income tax withholding (where applicable) - FICA (Social Security and Medicare) taxes - Any other applicable taxes or withholdings
Incentive compensation is reported as wages on Form W-2.
Tax Treatment: Incentive payments may be treated as supplemental wages and subject to supplemental wage withholding rates. Consult your tax advisor regarding the tax treatment of incentive compensation.
### 5.4 Payment Timing
The Company will make reasonable efforts to pay earned incentive compensation on the scheduled payment dates. However: - Payment may be delayed if additional time is needed to verify performance or resolve discrepancies - The Company is not liable for delays in payment beyond its control (system failures, data unavailability, etc.) - Late payment does not alter the amount owed, unless required by applicable law
State Wage Payment Laws: This plan complies with applicable state wage payment laws, which may specify: - Maximum time periods for payment of earned commissions - Requirements for payment upon termination - Other state-specific requirements
[Note: Specific compliance with state laws should be reviewed with legal counsel for each applicable jurisdiction]
### 5.5 Minimum Payment Thresholds
The Company may establish minimum payment thresholds (e.g., no payment if earned amount is less than $[amount]). Amounts below the threshold may be: - Carried forward to the next payment period - Forfeited (if specified in the plan) - Paid annually
### 5.6 Payment Currency and Method
Payments are made in U.S. Dollars via: - Direct deposit to the account you have designated for payroll - Or other method as specified by the Company
## 6. CLAWBACK, RECOUPMENT & ADJUSTMENTS
### 6.1 Overpayment Recovery
If you receive an overpayment for any reason, including but not limited to: - Calculation errors - Data errors - System errors - Incorrect quota or crediting - Failure to apply caps or thresholds - Any other error
The Company will recover the overpayment amount through: - Deduction from future incentive payments - Deduction from regular payroll (to the extent permitted by applicable law) - Direct repayment by you - Offset against amounts owed to you by the Company
Notice: The Company will provide you with notice of the overpayment and recovery method.
No Waiver: The Company’s failure to immediately seek recovery does not waive its right to recover overpayments.
### 6.2 Clawback for Post-Payment Events
Incentive payments are subject to clawback (recovery) if:
Customer-Related: - Customer cancels the order within [X] days of booking - Customer returns product within [X] days - Customer fails to pay (bad debt) - Sale is reversed or rescinded for any reason
Performance-Related: - Performance results are later determined to be inaccurate - Credit was improperly assigned - Performance did not meet quality standards
Conduct-Related: - Payment was obtained through fraud, misrepresentation, or misconduct - You violate Company policy - You are terminated for cause - You engage in activities harmful to the Company
Clawback Period: Clawback may occur up to [time period, e.g., “12 months”] following payment, or longer if required by law or if fraud is involved.
Recovery Method: Clawback will be effected through the methods described in Section 6.1.
### 6.3 Adjustments for Business Events
Incentive payments may be adjusted for: - Pricing corrections - Discount or rebate corrections - Revenue recognition adjustments - Returns, cancellations, or credits - Reclassification of sales (e.g., product category changes) - Inter-company sales adjustments - Other business adjustments
Adjustments may be retroactive and may result in additional payment or recovery of previous payments.
## 7. CREDITING & ATTRIBUTION
### 7.1 Sales Credit Rules
Sales are credited according to the crediting rules specified in your plan and the Company’s Sales Crediting Policy.
Company Discretion: The Company has sole discretion to determine: - Which participant(s) receive credit for a sale - The percentage of credit allocated to each participant - How credit is split in team selling situations - Treatment of sales in disputed or unusual circumstances
### 7.2 Territory & Account Assignment
Sales credit is generally based on territory and account assignments in effect at the time of the sale. However: - The Company may adjust credit for territory changes, account reassignments, or other circumstances - In-flight opportunities (sales in process at time of a change) may be credited according to specific rules in your plan or at Company discretion
### 7.3 Disputed Credits
If you dispute the crediting of a sale, you must submit a written dispute within [X days] of the date you became aware of the issue. See Section 13 (Disputes) for process.
## 8. CAPS, THRESHOLDS & LIMITS
### 8.1 Payout Caps
Most plans include maximum payout caps. When a cap applies: - You will not receive payment for performance above the cap - Performance above the cap does not carry over to future periods - Caps may be expressed as a percentage of target incentive, a dollar amount, or an attainment percentage
### 8.2 Payout Thresholds
Most plans include minimum performance thresholds. When a threshold applies: - You must achieve the threshold level of performance to earn any payout - Performance below threshold results in zero payout for that component or period - Threshold performance does not carry over to future periods (unless the plan explicitly permits)
### 8.3 Budget Caps
The Company may impose overall budget caps on total incentive payouts. If a budget cap is reached: - Individual payouts may be reduced pro-rata - Payout curves may be adjusted - Other adjustments may be made at Company discretion
Notice: The Company will provide notice if budget caps may affect your payout.
## 9. SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES
### 9.1 New Hires
If you are hired into an eligible position during a performance period: - Your quota will typically be pro-rated based on your start date - You may receive a draw, guarantee, or ramp period as specified in your offer letter or plan - Your eligibility begins on your start date or as otherwise specified
### 9.2 Transfers & Promotions
Internal Transfers: If you transfer to a different position: - You move to the incentive plan associated with your new position - Quotas and credits may be pro-rated between old and new positions - In-flight opportunities are treated according to plan rules or Company discretion - You must be in the new position for a minimum period (if specified) to be eligible
Promotions: Promotions are treated similarly to transfers. Your prior performance may or may not carry over to your new plan, depending on plan design and Company policy.
### 9.3 Demotions
If you are demoted to a position with a different incentive plan: - You move to the plan associated with your new position as of the effective date - Prior performance under the old plan may be paid out pro-rata or forfeited, depending on circumstances
### 9.4 Leave of Absence
Protected Leave (FMLA, Military, etc.): If you take legally protected leave: - Your eligibility and quota treatment will comply with applicable law - Upon return, your eligibility is reinstated - Quota and performance treatment during leave will be as required by law or as otherwise specified in your plan
Personal/Unpaid Leave: If you take a personal or unpaid leave of absence: - Your eligibility may be suspended during the leave period - Your quota may be adjusted - You may forfeit eligibility for periods during which you are not actively working - Specific treatment depends on leave type, duration, and Company policy
Return from Leave: Upon return from approved leave, your eligibility is reinstated according to your plan terms and applicable law.
### 9.5 Termination of Employment
Voluntary Resignation: If you resign: - You are eligible for payment only for performance periods completed before your last day of employment - Payment is pro-rated through your last day of employment - In-flight opportunities (sales not yet closed) are typically not credited to you - Payment timing is according to plan terms and applicable state law
Involuntary Termination (Not for Cause): If the Company terminates your employment without cause: - You are eligible for payment for performance periods completed before your last day - Payment is pro-rated through your last day - Treatment of in-flight opportunities depends on plan terms and circumstances - Payment timing is according to plan terms and applicable state law
Termination for Cause: If you are terminated for cause (including violation of Company policy, misconduct, poor performance, etc.): - All unpaid incentive compensation may be forfeited, at Company discretion - You are not entitled to payment for incomplete performance periods - All rights under any incentive plan are forfeited
Retirement: If you retire according to Company retirement policy: - Treatment may differ from standard voluntary resignation - Specific provisions may apply as defined in your plan or Company policy
Death: If you die while employed: - All earned but unpaid incentive compensation will be paid to your estate or designated beneficiary - Treatment of in-flight or incomplete performance periods will be according to plan terms or at Company discretion
Final Payment Timing: Final incentive payments upon termination are made: - According to the regular payment schedule, OR - As required by applicable state wage payment law, whichever is sooner
[Note: State laws vary significantly on commission payment timing upon termination - consult legal counsel]
### 9.6 Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)
If you are placed on a Performance Improvement Plan: - Your eligibility to participate in incentive plans may be suspended, at Company discretion - You may remain eligible with modified quota or terms - Upon successful completion of the PIP, eligibility may be reinstated - Upon termination during or following a PIP, you forfeit all unpaid incentive compensation

All plan designs, formulas, calculations, methodologies, and materials are proprietary and confidential to the Company. You may not: - Copy or reproduce plan materials except for your personal use - Share plan materials with competitors or external parties - Use Company compensation information for competitive purposes
