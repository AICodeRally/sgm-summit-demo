# PAYMENT TIMING POLICY
## Sales Compensation Payment Schedule & Requirements
Document Owner: Finance / Sales Operations Version: 1.0 Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Approved By: CFO, Compensation Review Board Last Updated: November 17, 2025 Next Review: January 1, 2027
## 1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes standard payment timing for all sales incentive compensation to ensure: - Compliance with state wage payment laws - Consistent participant experience - Adequate time for calculation and validation - Alignment with payroll cycles - Financial planning and accrual accuracy
## 2. SCOPE
Applies to all sales incentive compensation payments across all divisions and plans.
## 3. PAYMENT FREQUENCY STANDARDS
### 3.1 Standard Payment Frequencies
| Plan Type | Standard Frequency | Payment Timing |
| Transactional/High Volume | Monthly | By last business day of following month |
| Territory/Quota-Based | Quarterly | Within 30 days of quarter end |
| Annual Plans | Annually | Within 45 days of year end |
| SPIFs | At program end | Within 15 days of program close |

### 3.2 Rationale for Timing
Monthly: For high-transaction plans where participants need regular feedback and cash flow Quarterly: Balances administrative efficiency with timely payout for quota-based plans Annual: For long-cycle sales or strategic metrics requiring full-year assessment

## 5. DATA CUT-OFF REQUIREMENTS
### 5.1 Standard Cut-Off
Data Finalization: 5th business day of the month following performance period - All sales must be finalized in source systems (CRM, ERP) by cut-off - Finance validates revenue data - Sales Operations locks data for calculation
### 5.2 Late Transactions
Transactions after cut-off: - Included in next period’s payment - No retroactive adjustments to prior period (unless error - see Section 7)
Exception: Material transactions (>$X) may be included if: - Identified before calculation begins - Approved by Finance - Documented in payment memo
## 6. CALCULATION & VALIDATION TIMELINE
### 6.1 Standard Process Timeline
Example: Monthly Payment
| Day | Activity | Owner |
| Month End | Performance period closes | - |
| Day 1-5 | Data entry and finalization | Sales, Finance |
| Day 5 | Data cut-off | Systems |
| Day 6-8 | Extract data, run calculations | Sales Operations |
| Day 9-12 | Validate calculations, audit sample | Sales Ops + Finance |
| Day 13-15 | Resolve discrepancies | Sales Operations |
| Day 16-18 | Final review and approval | Finance Manager |
| Day 19-20 | Submit to Payroll | Sales Operations |
| Day 21-30 | Payroll processing | Payroll/Finance |
| Day 30 | Payment date | Payroll |

### 6.2 Expedited Timeline
For critical situations, timeline may be compressed with approval from Finance and Sales Operations leadership.
## 7. PAYMENT METHODS
### 7.1 Standard Method
Direct Deposit: All incentive payments made via direct deposit to account on file for regular payroll.
### 7.2 Payment Delivery
| Payment Type | Delivery Method |
| Regular monthly/quarterly incentive | Included in regular payroll run |
| Annual/year-end bonus | Separate payroll run or included in January payroll |
| SPIF payments | Separate payment or next regular payroll |
| Final payments (upon termination) | Per state law requirements |

### 7.3 Payment Statements
Participants receive electronic statement showing: - Performance period - Performance data - Calculation breakdown - Gross payment - Taxes withheld - Net payment
Statement Delivery: Available in [ICMT system / employee portal] by payment date.
## 8. TAX WITHHOLDING
### 8.1 Withholding Treatment
Incentive compensation is subject to: - Federal Income Tax: Supplemental wage rate (currently 22% for amounts up to $1M per year) - State Income Tax: Per state requirements - FICA: Social Security (6.2% up to wage base) and Medicare (1.45%, plus 0.9% additional Medicare tax for high earners) - Other: State disability, local taxes where applicable
### 8.2 Withholding Timing
Taxes withheld at time of payment, not when earned.
## 9. DELAYED PAYMENTS
### 9.1 Reasons for Delay
Payments may be delayed due to: - Data quality issues requiring additional validation - System failures - Calculation errors requiring correction - Pending dispute resolution - Legal hold or investigation - Exceptional business circumstances
### 9.2 Notification
If payment will be delayed: - Participants notified by [Sales Operations] at least 3 business days before scheduled payment date - Notification includes: Reason, expected resolution date, contact for questions
### 9.3 Expedited Make-Up Payment
Once delay is resolved: - Payment processed on next available payroll run - Or special off-cycle payment if delay is significant
No Interest: Company does not pay interest on delayed payments unless required by state law.
## 10. ADJUSTMENTS & CORRECTIONS
### 10.1 Post-Payment Adjustments
Reasons: - Customer cancellations or returns (within clawback period) - Data corrections - Calculation errors - Credit reallocation
Timing: - Adjustments made in next regular payment cycle - Overpayments recovered per Overpayment Recovery Policy - Underpayments paid in next cycle or expedited payment
### 10.2 Correction Process
Error identified (by participant, Sales Ops, Finance, or audit)
Investigation and root cause analysis
Calculation of correction amount
Approval by [Finance Manager]
Participant notification
Correction processed in next payment or expedited if material
## 11. STATE WAGE PAYMENT LAW COMPLIANCE
### 11.1 State-Specific Requirements
Earned Commissions: Most states require payment of “earned” commissions within a specified time after earning or termination.
State Variations: Requirements vary by state. Sales Operations maintains matrix of requirements for states where employees are located.
Example State Requirements:
| State | Regular Payment | Payment Upon Termination |
| California | Next regular payday after commission is earned and amount can be reasonably ascertained | Immediately if terminated involuntarily; next payday if resigned |
| New York | As per plan terms | Next regular payday |
| Texas | As per plan terms | Next regular payday |

[Note: This is illustrative only - consult legal counsel for specific state requirements]
### 11.2 Compliance Process
Sales Operations tracks employee locations
Payment timing designed to comply with most restrictive applicable state law
Legal reviews payment timing annually
Upon termination, state-specific rules applied
## 12. PAYMENT UPON TERMINATION
### 12.1 Voluntary Resignation
Timing: Per plan terms and applicable state law (typically next regular payment date)
Amount: - Pro-rated through last day of employment - Completed performance periods only - No payout for in-flight opportunities (unless plan specifies otherwise)
### 12.2 Involuntary Termination
Timing: Per applicable state law
Amount: - Per plan terms (may forfeit some or all unpaid incentive if terminated for cause) - Pro-rated through last day worked
### 12.3 Death
Timing: Within 30 days of notification Amount: All earned incentive paid to estate/beneficiary Payee: As designated in beneficiary forms or to estate
## 13. MINIMUM PAYMENT THRESHOLDS
### 13.1 Minimum Payment Amount
Threshold: $[50] per payment period
Rationale: Administrative efficiency
Treatment of Below-Threshold Amounts: - Carried forward to next period - Paid annually if cumulative amount remains below threshold - Paid upon termination regardless of amount
### 13.2 Exceptions
No minimum threshold for: - Final payments upon termination - Annual payment periods - Correction payments (underpayments)
## 14. ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES
| Role | Responsibility |
| Sales Operations | Calculate payouts, validate data, submit to payroll |
| Finance | Validate revenue data, approve calculations, manage accruals |
| Payroll | Process payments, withhold taxes, issue statements |
| Participants | Review statements, report discrepancies promptly |
| Managers | Support participants, escalate issues |

## 15. EXCEPTIONS & APPROVALS
Payment Timing Exceptions require:
| Exception Type | Approval Required |
| Expedited payment (off-cycle) | Finance Manager |
| Payment delay | Finance Manager + Sales Ops Leader |
| Payment more than 15 days late | CFO notification required |
| Waiver of minimum threshold | Finance Manager |
