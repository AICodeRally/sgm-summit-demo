# DATA RETENTION & ARCHIVAL POLICY
## Compensation Governance
Document Owner: Legal / IT / Governance Lead Version: 1.0 Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Legal Review: REQUIRED before implementation Last Updated: November 17, 2025 Next Review: Annual (January 2027)
## 1. PURPOSE
Establish consistent retention requirements for compensation-related documents and data to ensure: - Compliance with legal and regulatory requirements - Audit readiness and supportability - Appropriate data destruction - Balance between retention needs and cost/risk management
## 2. SCOPE
Applies to ALL compensation-related records in any format: - Electronic documents (Word, Excel, PDF, etc.) - System data (ICMT, CRM, ERP, HRIS) - Email communications - Paper records - Audio/video recordings (if any)

## 4. LEGAL & REGULATORY BASIS
### 4.1 Key Requirements
IRS: Minimum 3 years (typically 7 years for caution) SOX: Audit documentation for 7 years State Wage Laws: Vary by state (3-7 years) FLSA: 3 years (certain records) EEOC: 1 year (employment records) Securities Laws: 6-7 years (if applicable)
Conservative Approach: 7 years standard to cover all requirements
### 4.2 Legal Hold
Exception: If litigation, investigation, or audit is pending or reasonably anticipated: - STOP all destruction of potentially relevant records - Implement legal hold procedures - Consult Legal before any deletion - Maintain records until legal hold is lifted
## 5. RETENTION PERIOD CALCULATION
### 5.1 Start Date
Retention period begins: - Plan documents: End date of plan year - Participant data: Termination date or last payment date, whichever is later - Payments: Date of payment - Disputes: Date of resolution - Financial records: End of fiscal year - Audits: Date of audit report
### 5.2 Example Calculations
Example 1: - Plan Year: 2026 - Plan end date: December 31, 2026 - Retention period: 7 years - Destroy after: January 1, 2034
Example 2: - Participant termination: March 15, 2025 - Final payment: April 30, 2025 - Retention period: 7 years from final payment - Destroy after: May 1, 2032
## 6. STORAGE REQUIREMENTS
### 6.1 Active Storage (Current + 3 Years)
Systems: - ICMT (primary compensation system) - HRIS - Finance/ERP systems - Email systems
Access: Readily accessible by authorized users
### 6.2 Archive Storage (Years 4-7)
Systems: - Secure archive server - Cloud archive (if approved) - Offline backup media
Access: Retrievable within 5 business days upon request
Security: Encrypted, access-controlled
### 6.3 Paper Records
Current: On-site secure storage Archive: Off-site secure facility or converted to electronic and destroyed
Conversion: Paper may be scanned and destroyed after verification if: - Scanning meets quality standards - Electronic copy is legally equivalent - Index allows easy retrieval - Approved by Legal
## 7. DATA DESTRUCTION
### 7.1 Destruction Methods
Electronic Data: - Secure deletion using certified data wiping software - Destruction of physical media (drives, tapes) via certified vendor
Paper Records: - Shredding via certified vendor - Certificate of destruction obtained
### 7.2 Destruction Process
Annual Review (January each year): 1. IT/Records Manager identifies data/documents eligible for destruction 2. Legal reviews and confirms no legal holds 3. Governance Lead approves destruction list 4. Execute destruction 5. Document destruction (what, when, by whom) 6. Retain destruction certificates
### 7.3 Destruction Log
Maintain permanent log of: - Date of destruction - Description of records destroyed - Retention period that was applied - Authorization for destruction - Method of destruction - Certificate of destruction (if vendor used)
## 8. DATA PRIVACY CONSIDERATIONS
### 8.1 GDPR / CCPA
Right to Deletion: Participants may request deletion of personal data
Retention vs. Privacy: - Balance data privacy rights with legal retention obligations - Anonymize data where possible after business need ends - Document legal basis for retention when deletion requested
Process for Deletion Requests: 1. Participant submits request to [Privacy Contact] 2. Privacy/Legal reviews against retention obligations 3. If within retention period: Explain legal requirement to retain 4. If past retention period: Execute deletion and confirm to participant
### 8.2 Data Minimization
Principle: Retain only what is necessary
Implementation: - Purge unnecessary personal data even before retention period ends - Anonymize data sets when personal identifiers not needed - Aggregate data for analysis where possible
## 9. ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES

## 10. EXCEPTIONS & SPECIAL CASES
### 10.1 Permanent Retention
Retain permanently: - Corporate governance charters - Significant policy documents - Board-approved compensation programs (summary level)
### 10.2 Shortened Retention
May be destroyed earlier than 7 years (with Legal approval): - Duplicate copies (retain one official copy) - Drafts (after final version approved) - Routine communications with no legal significance
### 10.3 Extended Retention
Retain beyond 7 years if: - Required by specific law (e.g., certain securities matters) - Legal hold in effect - Historical significance for company - Approved by Legal for business reasons
## 11. AUDIT & COMPLIANCE
### 11.1 Internal Audit
Annual review: - Compliance with retention schedule - Proper archival of records - Timely destruction of eligible records - Adequacy of backup/recovery procedures - Security of archived data
### 11.2 External Audit Support
For external audits: - Provide records as requested - Demonstrate retention policy compliance - Show documentation is complete for audit period - Provide destruction logs if requested
## 12. TRAINING & AWARENESS
All employees handling compensation data must: - Understand retention requirements - Know how to identify records for legal hold - Follow proper procedures for archival and destruction - Report retention policy violations
Annual training required for: - Sales Operations - Compensation Team - Finance (Comp-related) - IT (Systems/Data)

## APPENDIX B: LEGAL HOLD PROCEDURE
If litigation/investigation is reasonably anticipated:
Legal issues legal hold notice to affected departments
Immediately suspend all destruction of potentially relevant records
Identify and preserve all relevant records (even past retention period)
Communicate hold to all custodians of relevant records
Monitor compliance with hold
Document all hold-related activities
When hold lifts: Resume normal retention schedule
Contact: Legal Department immediately if you become aware of potential litigation or investigation.
