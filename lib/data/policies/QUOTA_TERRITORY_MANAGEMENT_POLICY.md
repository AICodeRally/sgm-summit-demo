# QUOTA SETTING & TERRITORY MANAGEMENT POLICY

**Policy Code:** SCP-002
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** APPROVED
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes fair and transparent procedures for setting sales quotas and managing territory assignments. It ensures quotas are achievable, motivating, and aligned with business strategy.

---

## 1. PURPOSE & SCOPE

**Purpose:** Define quota-setting methodology, timing, adjustments, and territory management to ensure fairness and consistency.

**Scope:** All direct sales representatives in all segments (New Logo, Named, Upmarket, Emerging, QBR) and all regions.

---

## 2. QUOTA SETTING PRINCIPLES

- **Achievability:** Quotas are challenging but achievable for competent performers (60-70% of territory should reach 100%+)
- **Transparency:** Methodology and assumptions documented and shared
- **Fairness:** Quotas adjusted for territory size, market potential, and rep capability
- **Objectivity:** Based on data, not personal relationships
- **Consistency:** Similar territories receive similar quotas
- **Communication:** Set with sufficient advance notice (90 days minimum)

---

## 3. ANNUAL QUOTA SETTING PROCESS

### Timeline

| Activity | Timing | Owner |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Territory Analysis | Aug-Sept | Sales Ops |
| Historical Performance Review | Aug-Sept | Finance/Sales Ops |
| Market/Segment Potential Analysis | Sept | Sales Execs + Sales Ops |
| Draft Quotas by Manager | Sept-Oct | Regional Managers |
| SGCC Review & Adjustment | Oct | Sales Comp + CFO |
| Final Approval | Nov 1 | VP Sales + CFO |
| Manager Communication | Nov | Regional Leaders |
| Rep Communication | Nov | Managers (60+ days before effective) |
| Effective Date | Jan 1 | Finance/Payroll |

### Quota Setting Methodology

**Step 1: Territory Baseline Assessment**
- Historical territory performance (3 years)
- Current year performance trends
- Customer concentration analysis
- Market opportunity in territory

**Step 2: Segment-Specific Factors**

**New Logo:**
- New customer acquisition potential
- Green-field opportunities
- Channel partner capacity
- Market segment size

**Named Account:**
- Existing customer ARR (current)
- Expansion potential per customer
- Churn risk assessment
- Renewal probability

**Upmarket/Strategic:**
- Largest opportunity accounts
- Multi-year deal history
- Deal size and complexity
- Account growth trajectory

**Emerging:**
- High-volume, smaller-deal territories
- Market growth rate
- New product adoption
- Lower complexity deals

**Step 3: Regional Adjustments**
- Regional market growth/decline
- Competitive dynamics by region
- Regional economic factors
- APAC/EDU special considerations

**Step 4: Fairness Adjustments**
- Rep tenure and experience
- Territory maturity (ramp quotas for new territories/reps)
- Performance history
- Special circumstances (return from LOA, etc.)

**Step 5: Achievability Assessment**
- Upside potential (what % of top performers can achieve 150%+)
- Median performer target (60-70% should hit 100%+)
- Downside risk (what % at risk of 0%?)
- Adjustment if distribution not reasonable

### Quota Setting Meeting

Manager, Sales Ops, and Regional Leader discuss:
- Territory analysis and assumptions
- Proposed quota and rationale
- Comparable territories and quotas
- Rep capability assessment
- Market changes and adjustments
- Communication plan

**Documentation:** Minutes and quota assumptions filed

## 4. MID-YEAR QUOTA ADJUSTMENTS

**Trigger Events for Review:**
- Major market change impacting territory (>25% impact)
- Significant customer win/loss (>10% of quota)
- Territory reorganization/reassignment
- Rep change mid-year
- Competitive market shift
- Unforeseeable business event

### Adjustment Thresholds & Approval

| Adjustment Size | Requires | Timeline |
|-----------------|----------|----------|
| <10% adjustment | Manager + Regional Lead | 5 business days |
| 10-25% adjustment | Manager + Regional + Sales Ops | 10 business days |
| 25-50% adjustment | Manager + Regional + VP Sales | 15 business days |
| >50% adjustment | SGCC approval | 20 business days |

### Adjustment Process
1. Manager submits request with documentation
2. Review meeting with sales leadership
3. Approval per threshold
4. Rep notification and discussion
5. Document adjustment and rationale
6. Commission system update

**No adjustments made without documented business reason and approval.**

## 5. TERRITORY CHANGES & QUOTA IMPACT

### Territory Addition
- Quota increase: Add new territory's baseline quota
- Effective date: Date of territory assignment
- Ramp period: 60-90 days if accounts need transition
- Legacy revenue: Continue earning from legacy accounts during transition

### Territory Reduction
- Quota decrease: Proportional to territory size lost
- Transition credit: Legacy revenue credited during transition
- New territory of lost accounts: Rep receiving accounts gets quota increase

### Territory Realignment
- Quota adjusted to reflect new territory scope
- Historical baseline adjusted for territory changes
- Credit for transition period (up to 90 days)

### Territory Transfer Process
1. Identify affected reps and territories
2. Analyze quota impact
3. Calculate new quotas with transition terms
4. Approve new quotas
5. Communicate to reps involved
6. Honor legacy revenue during transition
7. System updates

**Example:**
- Rep A loses 20% of territory (assigned to Rep B)
- Rep A: Quota reduced 20%
- Rep B: Quota increased 20% (new territory quota)
- Rep A earns legacy revenue on lost accounts for 60 days
- After 60 days: Rep B earns full revenue on transferred accounts

## 6. QUOTA APPEALS & FAIRNESS REVIEW

**If rep disagrees with quota:**

### Step 1: Manager Discussion (14 days)
- Rep submits appeal with rationale
- Meet with manager to discuss
- If resolved, document agreement
- If not resolved, proceed to Step 2

### Step 2: Regional Review (14 days)
- Regional leader reviews appeal
- Compare to similar territories
- Assess market changes
- Make decision (approve, modify, or deny)
- Notify rep in writing

### Step 3: Fairness Committee (14 days, if needed)
- Composed of: VP Sales, VP Sales Comp, CFO
- Review comparable territories
- Independent assessment of fairness
- Final decision
- No further appeals after this level

**Timeline:** Complete appeal process within 45 days

## 7. SPECIAL QUOTA SITUATIONS

### New Territory / New Market Entry
- Ramp quota Year 1: 75% of mature territory quota
- Year 2: 90% of mature quota
- Year 3+: 100% of mature quota

### Returning from Leave of Absence (LOA)
- Quote pro-rated for period not on duty
- If LOA >60 days: Quota reduced proportionally
- Return quota: 80% of prior year, ramps to 100% by Q4
- Document LOA impact on quota

### Promotion/Role Change
- New role quota: Based on new role level and territory
- Transition period: 90 days at transitional quota (80% of target)
- After 90 days: Full quota for new role

### Merger or Acquisition Activity
- M&A integration may require temporary quota adjustments
- Approved by VP Sales + CFO
- Time-limited to integration period (typically 6 months)

### Macro Economic Events
- Market downturn: May trigger territory-wide adjustments
- Product launch: New opportunity quotas may be set
- Competitive pressure: Regional adjustments may be needed
- Requires SGCC approval for >10% impact

## 8. QUOTA COMMUNICATION & ENGAGEMENT

**90 Days Before Effective Date:**
- Manager meets with each rep to discuss quota and territory
- Explain methodology and assumptions
- Address questions and concerns
- Discuss rep capability and support needed

**30 Days Before Effective Date:**
- Final quota confirmed and communicated in writing
- Rep acknowledgment signed
- Plan documentation shared
- Training on new metrics (if changed)

**At Effective Date:**
- Compensation system updated
- First measurement begins
- Monthly tracking begins

---

## 9. QUARTERLY QUOTA MONITORING

**Monthly:**
- Sales Ops tracks progress toward quota
- Identify reps at risk of missing
- Manager coaching and support

**Quarterly:**
- Formal quota performance review
- Identify trends and patterns
- Consider mid-year adjustments if needed
- Communicate quarterly results

---

## 10. DOCUMENTATION & TRANSPARENCY

**Documentation Required:**
- Territory baseline analysis (historical data)
- Segment-specific quota assumptions
- Market analysis and adjustments
- Comparable territory matrix
- Final quota and approval
- Any adjustments and rationale
- Rep acknowledgment

**Transparency:**
- Rep can request to see comparable territories (with other reps' names redacted)
- Rep can request explanation of quota calculation
- Data provided within 5 business days
- Manager and Sales Ops available for discussion

---

## 11. SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS

**Salesforce:**
- Account assignment to rep (territory ownership)
- Territory and market segment tags
- Customer potential analysis

**Compensation System:**
- Quota assignment by month (Jan-Dec)
- Territory and segment classification
- Adjustment tracking and audit trail
- Quota vs. actual reporting

**Monthly Reporting:**
- Rep quota attainment %
- Territory performance vs. quota
- Segment performance analysis
- Comparative territory performance

---

## 12. APPEALS & DISPUTES

All quota disputes follow Dispute Resolution Procedures (See SCP-008).

**Dispute Escalation:**
1. Manager discussion (14 days)
2. Regional/Fairness Committee (14 days)
3. SGCC (if still unresolved)
